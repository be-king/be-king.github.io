TL;DR: I'm really excited about leveraging AI to make the electric grid cleaner, more efficient, and more reliable!

The electric grid on which all of modern life depends is racing towards the most consequential inflection point in its history. Clean energy needs to be added to the grid at unprecedented rates, electric vehicles and data centers are coming online causing massive surges in demand, extreme weather is increasing in severity and frequency, and the infrastructure holding it all together is long overdue for transformation. The tried and tested solutions of the past aren't going to hold against the complexity of the future grid: we're going to have to innovate our way out of this one.

While policy and investment will be essential, I believe that where I can most contribute is in the technical sphere by bridging the divide between electrical engineering expertise and emerging AI and information technology. My background in computational neuroscience, machine learning, and energy systems has given me a deep appreciation for complex, interconnected problems that exist at the intersection of fields, and I believe that the potential for innovation at this intersection is what's going to lead us to a cleaner, greener future.

At Leidos, I lead cross-functional AI teams building solutions for the energy sector. We leverage LLMs, computer vision, optimization, and agentic systems applied to grid modernization, transmission planning, and microgrid development. In addition to my role at Leidos, I'm also pursuing a Master of Science in Energy Policy and Climate Change at Johns Hopkins to deepen my understanding of the policy and engineering landscape shaping the grid's future.
